04 / Operating system

How I think.

P / 01

Prototypes over PRDs.

A working prototype kills more bad ideas in an afternoon than three weeks of docs ever will. I'd rather show a janky build than defend a polished spec.

P / 02

Real users over roadmaps.

Roadmaps are stories we tell stakeholders. Users are the truth. I'd rather be on a WhatsApp call with one real user than in a quarterly planning session with twelve.

P / 03

Data over rituals.

Sprint ceremonies don't move metrics. A funnel pulled at 11pm does. Decisions get made on evidence; rituals only run when they earn their keep.

P / 04

The model is the feature.

For AI products, prompt design, model choice, and the eval loop are the product. Treating them as "implementation details" is how AI demos die in production.

P / 05

Own the number.

If I can't tell you the metric, the baseline, and the move I'm making — I shouldn't be the PM on it. Accountability beats authority every single time.

P / 06

Kill weak ideas fast.

The cost of a bad idea isn't the build — it's the months you spend defending it. Prioritize ruthlessly. Sunk-cost reasoning is the most expensive bias in product.
